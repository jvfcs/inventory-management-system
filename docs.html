Introduction

Welcome to the Inventory Management System (IMS). This platform is a modern, Single-Page Application (SPA) designed to track hardware, deployable assets, and physical spaces.

While the backend database, automation, and API are powered entirely by Google Workspace (Google Sheets, Apps Script, and Google Drive), the frontend user interface is designed to be hosted externally on a free service like GitHub Pages or standard domain hosting. This architecture gives you an enterprise-grade web app with zero traditional server hosting costs.

UI & Navigation Features

The IMS is built as a Single Page Application, meaning it operates more like a native app on your phone than a traditional website. It is packed with quality-of-life features:

  • Deep Linking (URL Hashes): The app automatically updates your browser's URL as you navigate. You can safely bookmark, copy, and share links to specific sections (e.g., site.com/#dashboard) or specific items/locations (e.g., site.com/#item-12345). When a logged-in user clicks your link, they will be teleported straight to that view.
  • Sticky Header & Search: The top navigation and Global Search Bar are permanently "sticky". No matter how far down you scroll in a long list of items, the search bar and navigation buttons remain instantly accessible at the top of your screen.
  • Smart Navigation & Auto-Scroll: Whenever you click a search result, open a location, or switch between management tabs, the app automatically wipes the previous forms clean and snaps your screen back to the top so you are perfectly oriented.
  • Loading Screen: When you boot the app, it downloads your custom Theme, pulls your permissions, and fetches the entire inventory database to your device for lightning-fast usage.
  • The "Home" Button: Clicking the App Logo or App Title in the top left corner will instantly return you to the main Locations Grid.
  • Dark / Light Mode Toggle: Near the top right is a ☀️/🌙 switch. Clicking this repaints the app into a dark mode to reduce eye strain. The floating "Back to Top" arrow also dynamically adjusts its color to match your theme.
  • Collapsible Sections: Throughout the app, you will see headers with a ▼ icon. Clicking these headers will collapse or expand that section to keep your screen uncluttered.

Mobile App (PWA) Setup

The IMS is built as a Progressive Web App (PWA). This means you do not download it from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store. Instead, you "install" it directly from your web browser to your phone's home screen for a true native experience (full screen, no URL bars, optimized for notches/safe areas).

  • iPhone (iOS): Open your live hosted URL in Safari. Tap the Share icon (the square with an up arrow) at the bottom of the screen. Scroll down the menu and tap Add to Home Screen.
  • Android: Open your live hosted URL in Chrome. Tap the 3-dot menu in the top right corner. Tap Add to Home screen.

System Requirements

Because IMS is a modern Progressive Web App (PWA), it does not require a high-end computer or server. It runs entirely inside the user's web browser.

  • Desktop Browsers: Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Safari, or Firefox (Updated within the last 2 years).
  • Mobile Devices: iOS 14+ (Safari/Chrome) or Android 10+ (Chrome).
  • Network: A stable internet connection is required to sync with the Google Workspace backend. Offline mode is not currently supported.

Roles & Permissions (Dynamic RBAC)

IMS utilizes a Dynamic Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) engine. Instead of hardcoding permissions, Admins can create unlimited custom roles and instantly modify their capabilities using the visual matrix in the Users tab.

The Admin Safety Net & Self-Editing Lockout

To prevent accidental system lockouts and privilege escalation, the core Admin role is hardcoded with a permanent safety net, backed by API-level enforcements.

  • The Admin role cannot have its permissions unchecked in the matrix.
  • Only the core Admin can assign the Admin role to another user.
  • Only the core Admin can disable or demote another Admin.
  • Self-Editing Lockout: No user (not even an Admin) can edit their own role's permission matrix. This prevents rogue users from granting themselves unauthorized access.

Manage Users vs. Manage Roles

For strict enterprise compliance, assigning users to locations is completely separated from defining what those users can do.

  • Manage Users: Allows a user to add/disable employees and assign them to existing roles and existing LBAC groups. (Perfect for HR or Warehouse Managers).
  • Manage Roles: Allows a user to fundamentally alter the security matrix, granting or revoking system-wide capabilities like "Export Data" or "Perform Transactions". (Should be restricted to IT/System Admins).

Global Access vs. LBAC

In the Role Matrix, the very first checkbox is Global Access. If this is checked, the user bypasses all location restrictions and can see/manage the entire company's inventory. If this is unchecked, the user is subject to Location-Based Access Control (LBAC) and must be explicitly assigned to specific locations or groups by an Admin.

Location-Based Access Control (LBAC)

If a user's role does not have the Global Access permission checked, they are subjected to strict LBAC filters. If an Admin does not explicitly assign them to a Location, that location literally does not exist to them in the app. It will not appear on their dashboard, in their grid, or in their transaction dropdowns.

Access Groups

To make assigning locations easier, Admins can create "Access Groups" (e.g., West Coast, Arizona). When editing a location, you can tag it with a group. When assigning a user, you can assign them the entire group with one click, instantly granting them access to all locations bearing that tag.

Visibility Without Vulnerability (Management Tab)

In the Locations Management Tab, the system displays all locations to users with the 'Manage Locations' permission, even ones they don't explicitly own. This is a deliberate design choice to prevent users from unknowingly creating duplicate locations with slightly different names.

  • Read-Only Rows: If a user does not have LBAC permission for a location, that row will be visually dimmed. The inventory count will be hidden behind a 🔒 lock icon, and the Action buttons will be replaced with a "Read Only" badge.
  • Locked Access Groups: When a restricted user creates or edits a location, they will see a list of all Access Groups in the company. Groups they do not own will be greyed out and marked with a 🔒 lock icon. They cannot add or remove these locked tags, but the tags are safely preserved in the database.

Dashboard & Global State Toggle

The Management view features a unified State Toggle (Stock vs Deployed) at the top right of the screen. Flipping this switch instantly recalculates the entire dashboard, the Items catalog, and the Locations catalog to reflect the chosen state.

The Dashboard provides a real-time financial and operational snapshot based on the active toggle:

  • Global Snapshot: (Requires Global Access permission). Shows total unit counts and financial valuations across the entire company. Breaks down values by Physical Spaces vs Mobile Kits.
  • Location Groups Snapshot: Allows you to select an Access Group from a dropdown (e.g., "Arizona") and instantly recalculate the financial valuations and unit counts strictly for assets within that region.

Low Stock Alerts

If an item dips below its designated "Min Stock" threshold, two things happen:

  1. A global red warning banner appears at the very top of the app, visible from anywhere.
  2. Clicking that banner transports you to the Dashboard, where a red "Low Stock Warning" table details exactly which items are low and what their thresholds are.

Finding & Viewing Items

The main screen of the application is the Location Grid. Just like the management dashboard, this grid features a State Toggle at the top right. By default, it shows "Stock" locations. Flipping it to "Deployed" instantly recalculates the visual cards to show exactly which locations currently hold active field assets.

Clicking a location card opens it to reveal the detailed inventory currently associated with it.

Dual-State Locations: Inside every location, inventory is strictly separated into two tabs: Stock Inventory (items sitting on a shelf ready to be used) and Deployed Assets (items currently in use or active at that location).
  • Global "Everywhere" Location: At the end of the Physical Spaces grid is a special blue 🌎 card called "Everywhere". This is a system-generated master view. Clicking it will show you every single item across your entire company in one massive list.
  • Sorting: When viewing a location, use the dropdown in the top right to sort items alphabetically (A-Z, Z-A) or by Quantity (High-Low).
  • Grid vs. List View: Next to the sort dropdown is a physical toggle switch. Click it to swap between a visual "Grid" layout (large images, square cards) and a condensed "List" layout (small thumbnails, compact rows).
  • Image Zoom (Lightbox): If you click on an item's main image on its Detail Page, it will pop out into a high-resolution Lightbox overlay. Click the background to close it.
  • Quick Edit Shortcut: If your role has 'Manage Items' or 'Manage Locations' permission, you will see a small "✎ Edit" button under the image on detail pages. Clicking this instantly teleports you directly to the Management form for that specific entity, saving you time!

Global Search Engine

The Search Bar at the top of the app is incredibly powerful. As you type, the app searches through Barcodes, Make, Model, Descriptions, Notes, and Serial Numbers. Matches are highlighted in yellow. Click any result to jump directly to that item.

Wait, where did my item go? (Zero-Stock Visibility)
The main inventory grids strictly show items that currently reside in that specific location. If an item drops to 0 stock, it will completely disappear from that location's visual grid to keep things uncluttered. To find it and add new stock, simply use the Global Search Bar at the top of the screen!

Interactive Stock Badges (Teleporting)

When you view an Item's Detail page, you will see clusters of badges showing exactly where this item is currently located across your company. To prevent operational confusion, these are physically separated into Stock Locations (solid blue badges) and Deployed Locations (hollow outlined badges).

These badges are clickable! If you click one, the app will instantly teleport you to that specific location's inventory grid.

Note on LBAC: You will only see stock badges for locations you have permission to view. If the item exists in California, but you only have Arizona permissions, the California badges are completely hidden from you.

Asset Lifecycle (Move, Deploy, Return, Consume)

To execute a transaction, go to the Item Detail page. At the bottom, you will find the Action Form, which is split into two tabs depending on the item's current state.

  • Stock Actions:
    • Add / Remove: Adjust raw stock levels (e.g., receiving a new shipment, or throwing out old gear).
    • Move: Transfers stock from one location's shelf to another location's shelf.
    • Deploy: Deducts the item from Stock and moves it into the "Deployed Assets" ledger of the destination location.
  • Deployed Actions:
    • Return to Stock: Pulls the asset out of deployment and returns it to resting Stock at a specified location.
    • Consume / Remove: Permanently removes a deployed item from the system (e.g., the item broke on set, or was lost). Compliance Rule: Executing a Consume action strictly requires you to fill out the "Reason / Notes" text box for IT auditing purposes.

Bulk vs. Serialized Actions

Bulk Items (e.g., Cables, Screws)

Simply type in the quantity you wish to move, select the From/To locations, and click ADD, REMOVE, or MOVE. The system will math out the balances.

Serialized Items (e.g., Cameras, Laptops)

The quantity field is hidden. You must either click the checkboxes of the specific serials you are moving, or use the camera scanner to scan serial numbers into the text box.

Hardware Scanner Support!
If you have a physical USB or Bluetooth barcode scanner gun, it works natively with this app! Just click inside the Search Bar or Serial Input box and pull the trigger. The app's rapid-stroke engine will automatically capture the scan and drop to a new line without requiring you to hit 'Enter'.

Performing Audits

Audits are true-ups of physical stock compared to what the system expects. (Requires Permission: Perform Audits)

  1. Navigate to a Location. Important: Select either the "Stock Inventory" tab or the "Deployed Assets" tab first, depending on what you intend to count.
  2. Click the yellow Audit button in the top right. The audit will lock into whatever mode you selected (Stock or Deployed) to prevent mixed counting.
  3. The system will generate a list of everything it expects to find in that state, broken down by specific Spots/Bins.
  4. For Bulk Items: Simply type the actual number of items you count on the shelf. The row will turn yellow if there is a discrepancy.
  5. For Serialized Items: Click the "Audit" button. A modal will appear. Scan all physical serial numbers you find on the shelf. The system will cross-reference your scans, flag missing expected serials, and register unexpected found serials.
  6. Click Submit True-Up. The system will automatically generate Adjustment logs to fix the inventory balances.

Exporting Data (CSV)

The system allows you to easily extract data for external reporting or spreadsheets.

  • Location Inventory CSV: (Requires Permission: Export Data). Navigate to any Location (or the Global "Everywhere" view) and click the green Download CSV button in the top right. This generates a clean spreadsheet of every item, its category, exact spot/bin, quantity, and unit price for that specific location.
  • Transaction Logs CSV: (Requires Permissions: View Logs & Export Data). Go to Management > Logs. In the top right, there is a small input box and a green Download button. Enter the exact number of rows you want to export (e.g., 500, or 5000) and click Download. This exports the raw, chronological history of every movement in the system.

Management: Items (Catalog)

Requires Permission: Manage Items

The Items tab is where you define the catalog of equipment your company owns. In the data tables, click the column headers (blue underlined text) to instantly sort the database (including mathematically sorting the Total column to see your highest/lowest stocked items).

At-A-Glance Context: Item Descriptions and Notes are printed in small text directly underneath the Item Name in the table, saving you from having to click "Edit" just to read the fine print.
  • Barcode (Required): The most important field. Must be completely unique (e.g., a UPC code or internal SKU). The system will block the creation of duplicates.
    Admin Override & Database Cascade:
    By default, once an item is created, the barcode is permanently locked to preserve database integrity. However, Admins have the exclusive ability to edit existing barcodes. If an Admin changes a barcode, the backend engine automatically cascades this change throughout the entire database, updating all historical transaction logs to match the new barcode so no historical data is orphaned. Note: If the item has active stock, the system will prompt you for a final confirmation before initiating the cascade.
  • Make & Model: The manufacturer (e.g., Sony) and the product name (e.g., FX6 Camera).
  • Category: A high-level grouping used for filtering (e.g., CCTV, Cable, Accessories).
  • Tracking Type: Choose between "Bulk Quantity" (just count numbers) or "Individual Serials" (track exact units). Note: Do not change this after stock has been added, or your history will break!
  • Description: A brief overview of what the item is. This is globally searchable!
  • Unit Price: The financial cost of a single unit. The system uses this to calculate total inventory value on your dashboard.
  • Min Stock: The threshold at which this item triggers a "Low Stock" warning on the Dashboard.
  • Tech Specs URL: A link to a manual or website. A clickable link will appear on the Item Details page.
  • Image URL / File Upload: You can paste an image link from the web, or upload a file directly from your computer. Uploaded files are automatically saved to your private Google Drive and securely linked.
    Important Limit: Google Apps Script has strict payload limits. Do not upload raw, uncompressed photos (like 15MB 4K iPhone images). Keep uploaded image files under 3MB, or the database upload will timeout and fail. If you provide both an Image URL and a File Upload, the File Upload will override the URL.
  • Notes: Any special handling instructions. This is also globally searchable!

Enabling & Disabling Items

In the Items table, you will see a toggle button in the Actions column. Disabling an item removes it from searches and dropdowns so it can no longer be interacted with. Safety Net: The system will block you from disabling an item if there is still active stock in the system. You must remove/audit the stock to zero before retiring the item.

Management: Locations

Requires Permission: Manage Locations

The Locations tab is where you create the places your items reside. Like the items table, column headers are clickable for rapid sorting, including the dynamically calculated Count column.

At-A-Glance Context: Location Descriptions and Notes are printed directly underneath the Location Name in the table. On mobile devices, the action buttons automatically stack vertically to keep the table clean and readable.
  • Location Name: The name of the space (e.g., Warehouse A, Production Truck 1). Must be completely unique.
    Admin Override & Database Cascade:
    Like Barcodes, Location Names are locked for standard users once created. Admins have the exclusive ability to rename existing locations. Renaming a location automatically triggers a database cascade, rewriting all historical transaction logs to reflect the new name. Note: If the location currently holds active inventory, the system will warn you before proceeding.
  • Category: The general grouping (e.g., Office, Storage Room, Events).
  • Type: Choose between Physical Space (a permanent building) or Mobile Kit (an asset that moves and holds stock, like a Production Truck or Pelican Case).
  • Deployment Location: If you select "Mobile Kit" above, a new box appears asking where this kit is currently deployed to.
  • Access Groups (LBAC): A checklist of groups (e.g., Arizona, West Coast). Check the boxes to assign this location to those groups. You can quickly create new groups by typing a tag and clicking "Add".
  • Description & Notes: Add details about the location, access codes, or contact info.
  • Image URL / File Upload: Give the location a photo! Just like items, File Uploads override Image URLs.

Spots / Bins

You do not need to pre-create Spots/Bins. When a user is moving an item into a location on their device, they can simply type a new Spot name (e.g., "Shelf A3"), and the system will dynamically create it. To rename or delete an existing Spot, click the blue Spots button next to the location in the Location Management table.

Enabling & Disabling Locations

Like Items, Locations can be disabled. Safety Net: You cannot disable a location if it currently holds inventory.

Access Group Summary Dashboard

At the bottom of the Locations tab, you will see a visual dashboard. This automatically updates to show you every single Access Group in your system, and lists out exactly which locations belong to which groups, making it easy for Admins to verify their security architecture.

Management: Transaction Logs

Requires Permission: View Logs

The Logs tab provides an immutable, chronological history of every action taken in the system. By default, it loads the 20 most recent transactions to ensure the app stays lightning-fast and responsive.

If you need to look further back in time, simply scroll to the bottom and click the blue Load More Activities button to pull the next 20 records into the view.

Management: System Audit

Requires Permission: View SysLogs

In addition to standard inventory movement logs, the IMS features a permanent, immutable System Audit Trail. This tracks all administrative and configuration changes made across the platform.

  • Detailed Diffing: Whenever an Item, Location, or User is updated, the backend engine automatically compares the old data against the new data and records the exact changes (e.g., Min Stock: 5 -> 10, Role: Operator -> Manager).
  • Click for Details: In the System Audit tab, you can click on any row to open a detailed popup modal showing exactly who performed the action, when, and the specific text diff of what was changed.
  • CSV Export: Like transaction logs, System Audits can be downloaded directly to a CSV file for compliance, security reviews, and accountability tracking.

Management: Users

Requires Permission: Manage Users

The Users tab controls who can log in. If an email is not in this list, they will hit an "Access Denied" screen.

Security Hierarchy Enforcement
While Sub-Admins have access to this tab to manage Managers, Operators, and Viewers, they are strictly prohibited by the API from creating new Admins/Sub-Admins, or modifying/disabling existing Admins/Sub-Admins. Only a full Admin can manage protected top-level roles. Furthermore, Admins cannot accidentally or maliciously demote or disable other Admins.
  • Full Name (Optional): By default, the system tries to guess a user's display name from their email address (e.g., john.doe@company.com becomes "John Doe"). If their email is weird (e.g., admin123@company.com), you can type their actual name here to override the system guess!
  • Email Address: Their exact Google login email.
  • Role: See the "Roles & Permissions" section for details on access levels.
  • Assign Locations (LBAC): If the user is a Restricted role, a checklist will appear. Check the Groups or specific Locations they are allowed to access.

Employee Offboarding (Disabling vs. Deleting)

When an employee leaves the company, you must Disable them using the button in the UI table. Do not open the Google Sheet and delete their row entirely.

  • Disabling (Correct): Instantly rejects their login tokens and revokes Web App access, but keeps their name and email in the database so historical transaction logs and audits still show their proper display name.
  • Deleting Row (Incorrect): If you delete their row from the Google Sheet, all historical logs tied to their email will lose their relational mapping and revert to showing "Unknown" or just the raw email address.

Safety Net: The system will prevent you from accidentally disabling your own Admin account.

Background Sync Engine

To ensure all users across the company see accurate stock numbers without ever having to refresh their web browser, the app utilizes an intelligent, non-intrusive Background Sync Engine.

  • Auto-Soft Refresh: Every 15 seconds (configurable in the Theme tab), the app silently asks the Google database for changes. It injects new numbers, checks if an Admin revoked your permissions, and updates LBAC assignments automatically.
  • Sync Indicator: When this happens, a tiny black badge containing a spinner and the text "Syncing..." will gently fade in at the bottom right corner of your screen, then fade away.
  • Zero Interruption: This sync happens in the background. It will not interrupt you if you are typing in a form or scanning a barcode.

Administration: Theme & Branding

Requires Permission: Manage Theme

The Theme tab allows Admins to completely white-label the software without ever touching code. Here is a breakdown of every field:

  • Company Name: Legal entity name. This is automatically injected into the dynamically generated Legal/Privacy Modal in the footer.
  • Developer Name: Name of the primary architect (injected into the Legal Modal).
  • Title: The full name of your platform (e.g., "Inventory Management System"). Displays in the browser tab and on desktop web views.
  • Short Title: An abbreviation (e.g., "IMS") used on small mobile phone screens where space is tight.
  • Use Short Title on Login?: Toggle this to "Yes" if you want the big login screen to say "IMS Login" instead of "Inventory Management System Login".
  • Login Screen Message: An optional text field that appears directly above the Google Sign-in button. Perfect for internal company notices, IT helpdesk instructions, or disclaimer text.
  • Currency Symbol: Set this to $, €, £, or any symbol. It automatically formats all financial math across the app.
  • Footer Text: The small copyright text at the very bottom of the app.
  • Admin Contact: The email address shown to users if they hit the "Access Denied" screen, telling them who to contact for access.
  • Logo Image URL: A web link to your company logo. This displays on the login screen, loading screen, and top navigation bar.
  • Default 'No Image' Graphic: If an item doesn't have a photo, the app generates a gray "No Image" box. You can replace that with a custom branded placeholder by pasting an image URL here.
  • Google Client ID & Drive Folder ID: The critical connection strings required for Google Login and Image Uploads.
  • Sync Interval: How often the Background Sync Engine silently pings the database (in seconds). Default is 15.
  • Idle Timeout (Minutes): A security feature that automatically wipes the user's session and kicks them back to the login screen if no mouse movement, scrolling, or typing is detected for X minutes. Set to 0 to disable.
  • Light Mode / Dark Mode Palettes: Define Primary Brand Colors, Background Colors, Card Colors, and Text Colors for both display modes. When a user toggles the sun/moon icon, the app repaints itself instantly using these rules.

Deployment Step 1: Google Drive Setup

The system needs a secure place to store the photos you upload of your items and locations.

  1. Open your Google Drive.
  2. Create a new folder and name it something like "IMS Images".
  3. Double click the folder to open it.
  4. Look at the web address (URL) at the top of your browser. It will look like this:
    https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1aBcD2eFgH3iJkL4mNoP5qRsTuV6wXyZ
  5. Copy the long string of letters and numbers at the very end. That is your Drive Folder ID. Paste it somewhere safe (like a notepad).
Security Note: Do not share this folder publicly. Keep it restricted to yourself. The backend script runs under your credentials and will securely upload images here for you!

Deployment Step 2: Google Login Setup

To allow users to log into your app securely using their Google accounts, we need to create a "Client ID" in the Google Developer Console.

  1. Go to the Google Cloud Console and sign in.
  2. Click the project dropdown in the top left and select New Project. Name it "IMS Login" and click Create.
  3. In the left sidebar, navigate to APIs & Services > OAuth consent screen.
  4. Select Internal (if you only want employees in your company's Google Workspace to use it) or External (if you want anyone with a Gmail account to be able to use it), and click Create.
  5. Fill out the required App Information (App name, support email) and click Save and Continue through the remaining steps.
  6. In the left sidebar, click Credentials.
  7. Click + CREATE CREDENTIALS at the top and select OAuth client ID.
  8. Select Application type: Web application. Name it "IMS Web App".
  9. Under Authorized JavaScript origins, click + ADD URI. You must enter the exact web address where your user interface will eventually be hosted.
    Example: https://yourusername.github.io
    Important: Do not include trailing slashes or file paths (NO /index.html at the end).
  10. Click Create. A popup will appear with your Client ID. It will look something like this:
    1234567890-abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz.apps.googleusercontent.com
  11. Copy this Client ID and save it to your notepad!

Deployment Step 3: Database Setup

Now we will build the actual database using Google Sheets. Accuracy is critical here! Do not delete columns once established, as it can skew historical indexing.

  1. Create a brand new, blank Google Sheet. Name it "IMS Database".
  2. At the bottom of the screen, you will see "Sheet1". Double click it to rename it. You need to manually create seven tabs with specific names. (The system will automatically generate an 8th tab called "Roles" later). Use the "+" button to add new tabs.
  3. Create these 7 tabs and set up the exact Headers/Formulas required in Row 1:
    • Tab 1: Items
      Type Headers (A1 to M1): Barcode, Make, Model, Category, Min, Spec, Image URL, Status, Description, Price, Image File, Notes, Track Serials
    • Tab 2: Locations
      Type Headers (A1 to J1): Location Name, Type, Status, Description, Notes, Image URL, Image File, Space Type, Deployment Location, LBAC Groups
    • Tab 3: Log
      Type Headers (A1 to J1): Date, Email, Barcode, From, To, Quantity, Action, Serial Number, Spot, Notes
    • Tab 4: Users
      Type Headers (A1 to G1): Email, Last Login, Status, Role, Name, Theme, Locations
    • Tab 5: Template
      Type Headers (A1 to B1): Key, Value
    • Tab 6: Dashboard
      Paste this EXACT formula into cell A1: 
      =IFERROR(QUERY({ARRAYFORMULA(TO_TEXT(Log!C1:C)), ARRAYFORMULA(TO_TEXT(Log!E1:E)), Log!F1:G}, "SELECT Col1, Col2, SUM(Col3) WHERE Col1 <> '' GROUP BY Col1, Col2 PIVOT Col4 LABEL Col1 'Barcode'"), "No Transaction Data Found")
    • Tab 7: Alerts
      Paste this EXACT formula into cell A1: 
      =ARRAYFORMULA(QUERY({Items!A2:B, Items!E2:E, IFERROR(VLOOKUP(Items!A2:A, QUERY(Log!A:G, "SELECT C, SUM(F) GROUP BY C"), 2, FALSE), 0)}, "SELECT Col1, Col2, Col3, Col4 WHERE Col4 < Col3 AND Col1 IS NOT NULL LABEL Col1 'Barcode', Col2 'Item', Col3 'Min Threshold', Col4 'Total On Hand'"))
  4. The Most Important Step: Go to your newly created Template tab.
    In cell A2, type EXACTLY: googleClientId
    In cell B2, paste the long Client ID you got in Step 2!
    In cell A3, type EXACTLY: driveFolderId
    In cell B3, paste the short Folder ID you got in Step 1!

Deployment Step 4: The Payload Vault

For maximum security, IMS uses a Dynamic Injection Architecture. The actual application code is never hosted publicly. Instead, it is kept in a secure vault on Google's servers and only sent to users who successfully log in.

  1. While looking at your Google Sheet, click Extensions > Apps Script at the top.
  2. Click the + icon next to "Files" on the left and select HTML. Name the file exactly payload.
  3. Open the payload.html file provided to you, copy everything inside, and paste it into this new file. This file contains 90% of the application's CSS, HTML, and Javascript.
  4. Click the 💾 Save icon.

Deployment Step 5: API Setup & External Auth

Now we attach the API logic to serve the data. Because this system uses enterprise-grade cryptographic token verification, you must explicitly grant Google Apps Script permission to talk to Google's external OAuth servers.

  1. In Apps Script, click on the Code.gs file on the left sidebar. Erase the default text.
  2. Open the Code.gs file that was provided to you, copy all of the text, and paste it into the editor.
  3. Click the 💾 Save icon.
  4. AUTHORIZE EXTERNAL REQUESTS (CRITICAL STEP):
    • Scroll to the very bottom of the code and temporarily paste this code:
      function forceAuth() { UrlFetchApp.fetch("https://google.com"); }
    • At the top toolbar of the editor, click the dropdown next to "Debug", select forceAuth, and click Run.
    • A popup will say "Authorization Required". Click Review Permissions > Select your account > Click Advanced > Click Go to project (unsafe) > Click Allow.
    • Your script is now legally allowed to verify login tokens! (You can now delete the temporary forceAuth function and save again).

IT Compliance & Data Integrity

Target Audience: Infrastructure / IT Administrators & Compliance Auditors

For organizations undergoing SOC-2 internal audits or strict compliance reviews, the IMS architecture is designed with multiple layers of enterprise security.

1. Cryptographic Identity Verification (Anti-Spoofing)

The backend API operates on a Zero-Trust model. The frontend does not simply pass an email address to request data. Instead, it passes the raw, encrypted JSON Web Token (JWT) generated by Google upon login. On every single API request, the backend independently pings Google's OAuth 2.0 servers to mathematically verify the cryptographic signature of the token before executing any database reads or writes.

2. Concurrency Locks (Double-Spend Protection)

To prevent database corruption resulting from asynchronous race conditions (e.g., two warehouse workers clicking "Deploy" on the exact same item at the exact same millisecond), the API utilizes Google's LockService. All write-actions are forced into a single-file queue. If a transaction is actively writing to the database, subsequent requests are paused for up to 10 seconds to read the newly updated stock levels before proceeding.

3. XSS (Cross-Site Scripting) Sanitization

To prevent malicious payload execution, all user-generated text inputs (Item Notes, Descriptions, Location Names, and Spot names) are passed through a strict client-side HTML sanitizer before being injected into the DOM. This neutralizes executable tags (<script>, <iframe>) while preserving safe string data.

4. Securing the Underlying Database (Crucial)

The Google Sheet acts as the raw database. Do not give IMS App Admins "Editor" access to the Google Sheet. To preserve the strict immutability of the SysLog and Transaction Logs, the Google Sheet should only be shared with standard Admins as Viewers. The Web App operates via a Service Account ("Execute as: Me"), granting it write-access without requiring the user to have native Google Drive write-access.

5. Disaster Recovery & Rollbacks

Because the database is a Google Sheet, you have access to enterprise-grade disaster recovery built-in. If a rogue IT Admin accidentally mass-deletes rows, open the Google Sheet, click File > Version history > See version history, and restore the document to the exact second before the corruption occurred.

6. Dynamic Code Injection & Obfuscation

The IMS utilizes a zero-trust front-end architecture. If a user views the source code of the public URL, they will only see a lightweight shell containing the login button. Proprietary operational workflows and API structures are securely stored on Google servers and are only injected into the browser's memory if the user passes the JWT authentication.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

This system is incredibly resilient, but because it relies on a Google Sheet as its brain, certain manual actions by Admins can break it. Avoid these common pitfalls:

1. Do not rename tabs or delete columns in the Google Sheet.
The backend API specifically looks for tabs named "Items", "Locations", "Users", etc., and searches for specific column headers. You can resize columns, and you can add new rows, but renaming a tab or deleting the "Barcode" header will instantly crash the app.
2. Do not change an item's Tracking Type after it has stock.
If you create an item as "Bulk Quantity", log 50 of them into a location, and then later edit the item to be "Individual Serials", the app will be completely confused because those 50 items don't have serial numbers attached to them. If you must change tracking types, audit the item down to 0 stock first.
3. Never change Barcodes or Location Names directly in the Google Sheet.
Always use the IMS web interface (as an Admin) to edit a barcode or rename a location. The web interface triggers a script that safely updates all associated transaction logs. If you manually type over a barcode or location name directly in the Google Sheet's "Items" or "Locations" tabs, you will permanently sever its connection to its history in the "Logs" tab.
4. Invalid Roles in the Database trigger a Security Failsafe.
The system authentication engine is strictly case-insensitive but enforces exact role matching. If a user is assigned an unknown or misspelled role directly in the Google Sheet (e.g., "Supervisor", "Read-Only", "viewerrr"), the system will automatically trigger a Security Failsafe. The user's login token will be rejected, and they will be permanently locked out with an "Access Denied" screen until an Admin assigns a valid role from the dropdown menu in the UI.
5. If you edit the Apps Script (Code or Payload), you MUST create a New Deployment.
If you ever edit Code.gs or payload.html, simply clicking the "Save" icon is not enough. Google caches the API. You must click Deploy > Manage Deployments > Edit (Pencil Icon) > New Version > Deploy for your code changes to actually go live.
6. Duplicating variables between the Shell and the Payload will break the app.
Because index.html (the Shell) and payload.html (the Vault) ultimately run in the same browser window, you cannot declare global variables (like let appCurrency or const GAS_URL) in both places. If you do, the browser will throw an "already declared" error and fail to boot. Keep logic strictly separated.

Troubleshooting & FAQ

Help! The app is stuck on "Loading Database..."

This almost always means the GAS_URL inside your index.html file is incorrect, or you failed to deploy the Google Sheet correctly.

  • Did you deploy the Apps Script as a "Web App"?
  • Is the access level set to "Anyone"?
  • Does the URL you pasted into index.html end in /exec? (If it ends in /dev, it will fail for everyone except you).

I'm stuck in an infinite Login Loop / "Session Expired" immediately.

If you successfully sign in with Google, see the loading spinner, but are immediately kicked back to the login screen, your backend API is failing to verify the token.

  • The UrlFetchApp Error: The most common cause is that you forgot Step 4 in the API Setup. The Google Apps Script does not have permission to ping the external internet to verify the token. You must create the `forceAuth()` function, run it in the editor, and accept the "External Request" permissions.
  • 1-Hour Expiry: Google cryptographic tokens expire exactly 60 minutes after generation. If you leave the app open in a background tab overnight, your next click will gracefully boot you to the login screen to refresh your token.

The Google Login button isn't appearing!

If the login screen appears but the actual "Sign in with Google" button is missing, your web browser is blocking the script.

  • Ensure you do not have an aggressive Ad-Blocker turned on for this site.
  • Browsers like Brave, Safari, or Firefox in "Strict Privacy" mode block 3rd-party cookies. Google Login requires these to function. Click the shield icon in your URL bar and disable tracking protection for your app's specific URL.
  • Ensure you properly pasted the Google Client ID into the Template tab of your Google Sheet.

My uploaded images are showing up as broken gray squares.

This is a strict IT policy issue within Corporate Google Workspace accounts.

  • The app uploads images to your Google Drive and sets their permission to "Anyone with the link can view".
  • If your company's IT Administrator has configured Google Workspace to "Prevent users from sharing files outside the organization", Google Drive will block the image from loading on your external website.
  • The Fix: Ask your IT Admin to allow external link sharing for the specific account hosting the Drive Folder, or use a standard free @gmail.com account to host the database instead of a corporate account.

My camera scanner isn't turning on.

Modern browsers strictly block camera access on unsecured connections.

  • Make sure the website you are hosting the app on uses https://, not http://. GitHub Pages handles this automatically.
  • Check your browser settings (or your phone's Settings > Safari/Chrome) to ensure you haven't accidentally denied camera access for this specific website.

I hit an "Access Denied" screen.

The email you used to log into Google is not listed in the Users tab of the database.

  • If you are the Admin, open your Google Sheet and ensure your email is typed perfectly in the Users tab with the Status set to "Active".
  • If you are an employee, contact your system administrator to have your email added.

I updated the UI, but I still see the old version.

Web browsers love to cache (save) HTML files to make them load faster. If you deployed a new version of your index.html file but aren't seeing the changes, force your browser to fetch the new file:

  • Windows/PC: Press Ctrl + F5 or Ctrl + Shift + R.
  • Mac: Press Cmd + Shift + R.
  • Mobile: Open your browser settings and clear the website data/cache.

How do I back up my entire database?

Because your entire backend lives inside Google Workspace, creating a hard backup takes three seconds and requires no technical knowledge.

  • Open your "IMS Database" Google Sheet.
  • Click File > Download > Microsoft Excel (.xlsx).
  • You now have a complete, offline, hard-copy backup of every item, location, user, and transaction log in your system! We recommend doing this once a month.

How do I print Barcode/QR Code stickers?

The IMS is designed to read and track barcodes, but it does not directly communicate with desktop printers to print them.

  • You can use any standard label maker (like a Dymo, Zebra, or Brother printer) and its included software to generate and print your physical stickers.
  • Simply type the Barcode numbers you generated into the IMS "Barcode" field, and the scanner will instantly recognize them!

I made a mistake. How do I delete a transaction log?

You can't, and that is by design! The system operates on an Immutable Ledger.

  • To prevent employee theft or "ghost inventory" manipulation, transaction history cannot be deleted or altered once submitted.
  • If an operator accidentally moves 50 cables to the wrong truck, they simply need to execute a new transaction moving those 50 cables back to the correct location. Both the mistake and the correction will be permanently logged for full accountability.

How do I disconnect my Google Account from this app?

If an employee leaves the company, or you simply wish to revoke the app's access to your Google profile, you can do this directly through Google's security portal.

  • Go to myaccount.google.com/permissions.
  • Find the "IMS Web App" (or whatever you named your project in Step 2 of deployment) in the list of Third-Party apps.
  • Click it and select Remove Access. The app will immediately lose the ability to authenticate your email.
